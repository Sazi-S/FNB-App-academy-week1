About Thailand

Thailand is a Southeast Asian country known for its rich culture, tropical beaches, opulent royal palaces, ancient ruins, and ornate temples displaying figures of Buddha. here are some key aspects of Thailand:

  • Never-ending New Year: Thailand celebrates three New Years — the international one on January 1st, Chinese New Year in January/February, and Songkran, the Thai New Year in April, known for its massive water fights
  • Land of Smiles:Thailand is often called the "Land of Smiles" because Thai people are known for their friendly and welcoming nature.
  • Unusual Laws: It's illegal to step on Thai currency, as it features the image of the King, who is highly revered.
  • Monks with iPhones: It's not unusual to see Buddhist monks using smartphones or even social media. Thailand’s monks live in a modern world too!
  • The World's Longest City Name: Bangkok’s full ceremonial name is: "Krung Thep Maha Nakhon Amon Rattanakosin Mahinthara Ayutthaya Mahadilok Phop Noppharat Ratchathani Burirom..." (and it goes on!). It holds a Guinness World Record
  • Home to the World's Smallest MammalThe bumblebee bat (also known as Kitti’s hog-nosed bat), found in Thailand, is the smallest mammal in the world.
  • Unique greetings: Instead of handshakes, Thai people greet with a “wai” — placing hands together like a prayer and bowing slightly.
  • Red Bull Was Inspired by Thailand:The original energy drink was a Thai brand called Krating Daeng, which inspired the global Red Bull brand
  • More Temples than You Can Count: Thailand has over 40,000 Buddhist temples, many of which are still active today.
  • No Last Names Until 1913:Thais only began using surnames after a law passed in 1913 requiring them.
